Production Techniques as Teaching Tools
EQ / Timbre
Frequency shaping · Spectral analysis
Theory: EQ shapes timbre — the color of sound. Boosting ~3kHz adds vocal punch; cutting <100Hz removes mud. Same as choosing chord voicings — every frequency decision is intentional.
Swing / Quantize
Groove · Timing · Human feel
Groove 0%
Theory: Quantization snaps notes to the grid (meter). Swing shifts even 16ths forward, creating the push-pull feel of boom-bap. It's syncopation made visible — the off-beat arrives just late, adding tension.
Sampling
Layering · Arrangement · Recontextualization
Theory: Sampling is arrangement in real time. Each layer occupies frequency space. Stack a bass sample, a chord stab, and a drum break — you're building texture, harmony, and rhythm simultaneously.
Waveform / Dynamics
Amplitude · Attack · Decay
Theory: Waveforms visualize dynamics — the attack is the left spike, the decay is the tail. A kick's short tail vs a pad's long release is the difference between staccato and legato in classical terms.
